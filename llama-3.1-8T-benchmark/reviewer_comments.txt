I would recommend reworking Fig 2 on the LLama-3.1-8b experiments.
The y-axis scaling on every subfigure is changing, making side-by-side
comparisons of the results difficult to compare easily. Moreover, the
overall ranges of the y-axis data across all figures isn't substantially
different, so a fixed y-axis range may be reasonable to consider. You may
even want to consider consolidating each row of plots into a single plot
for even more clarity and to potentially create space for the matrix
function benchmark data. Also, why did you compare quantized FP8 inference
performance on the Gaudi2 against non-quantized BF16 inference performance
on A100? That's not quite a fair comparison. If you have the baseline
BF16 performance on Gaudi2 available, it should also be included as well.

The LLM benchmark comparison between Gaudi2 and A100 is not apples-to-apples. The Gaudi2 runs use FP8 quantized inference while the A100 runs use BF16 without quantization. The paper acknowledges this in the methodology but does not address the impact on the comparison. A fairer evaluation would include BF16 results on the Gaudi2 as well, or at minimum discuss the expected effect of quantization on the throughput and energy numbers. As presented, the speedup and energy-per-token figures in Figure 2 conflate hardware differences with precision differences.
